What patient-facing privacy governance can look like.

This public showcase illustrates how a hospital portal can make notice, consent, transparency, rights, nomination and grievance workflows visible to a Data Principal. It is a design and governance demonstration—not a live hospital system or legal-compliance certification.

Demonstration scope

  • Patient-readable, purpose-specific notice architecture
  • Separate optional and non-consent processing pathways
  • Consent evidence and withdrawal journey
  • Access, correction, erasure and grievance intake
  • Recipient transparency and escalation design

Control architecture

The design translates statutory and operational requirements into visible patient journeys. Production implementation would additionally require legal validation, security engineering, system integration, accessibility testing and operating procedures.

01

Independent notice

Itemised data categories, specified purposes, retention statements, recipient classes and contact channels are presented before consent.

02

Purpose-level choice

Optional purposes are separated from treatment, emergency, statutory and other permitted processing grounds.

03

Evidence trail

The interface demonstrates notice versioning, timestamps, selected purposes and a consent-receipt concept.

04

Rights workflow

Access, correction, updating, completion and erasure requests are routed through identity verification and case tracking.

05

Transparency register

Patients can see recipient categories, processing roles, shared-data descriptions and the asserted processing basis.

06

Redressal and escalation

Grievance intake, response commitments, status visibility and escalation routes are incorporated into the patient journey.

Illustrative lifecycle

A practical implementation treats consent as one component of a broader data-governance lifecycle.

1 · NoticeExplain data and purposes.
2 · ChoiceCapture optional selections.
3 · EvidenceRecord version and receipt.
4 · ServiceEnable access and requests.
5 · RedressalTrack and escalate issues.

Important governance qualification

Interface design alone does not establish compliance. A production deployment must be supported by data mapping, lawful-basis analysis, sectoral-law overlays, processor contracts, access controls, breach response, retention enforcement, audit evidence, cybersecurity controls and accountable operating ownership.
